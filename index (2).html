Newton and the Discovery of Gravity

Introduction

Sir Isaac Newton, a brilliant mathematician and physicist, is credited with the discovery of gravity. Learn more about this fascinating journey below.

Background

Newton's work on gravity began in the late 17th century. Inspired by the fall of an apple, he started contemplating the force that pulls objects towards the Earth.

The Experiment

Newton conducted thought experiments and observed the motion of celestial bodies. He formulated the law of universal gravitation, which states that every mass attracts every other mass in the universe with a force proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between their centers.

Contribution to Science

Newton's discovery revolutionized our understanding of the natural world. The law of universal gravitation became a fundamental principle of physics and remains crucial in explaining various phenomena.
